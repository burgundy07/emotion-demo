Synthesis of Emotional Speech: Comparing Baseline, Pitch Control, and Fine-tuning in FastSpeech2

Author:Hanyu Zhang

Email:zzhanghd01@gmail.com

Overview

This project investigates the expressiveness of synthesized emotional speech by applying three different methods within the FastSpeech2 framework. We compare the performance of (1) a neutral baseline model trained on LJSpeech, (2) pitch-duration adjustment during inference, and (3) fine-tuning on emotional speech datasets (ESD Happy / Sad) under low-resource conditions. The aim is to explore which method produces more emotionally expressive and natural-sounding speech.

Methodology

For each sentence, we synthesized four versions: (1) Baseline (LJSpeech), (2) Pitch & Duration Adjusted version (manually simulated emotion), (3) Emotion-specific from-scratch training (ESD), and (4) Fine-tuned on emotional data using the LJSpeech checkpoint.
