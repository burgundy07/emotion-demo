Synthesis of Emotional Speech: Comparing Baseline, Pitch Control, and Fine-Tuning in FastSpeech2

Author: Hanyu Zhang

Overview

This project investigates the emotional expressiveness of synthesized speech using the FastSpeech2 framework. The objective is to evaluate which modeling strategy yields speech that is more natural and emotionally expressive to human listeners. Four approaches are compared: (1) a neutral baseline model trained on LJ-Speech, (2) pitch and duration scaling applied at inference to simulate emotions, (3) emotion-specific models trained from scratch using a small emotional corpus, and (4) fine-tuning a pre-trained model on limited emotional data representing three target emotions (happy, sad, angry).

  1. Baseline: A neutral TTS model trained only on non-emotional LJSpeech data.
  2. Pitch–Duration Adjustment: Emotional tone simulated at inference by scaling F0 and duration.
  3. From Scratch: Emotion-specific models trained from scratch using a subset of ESD.
  4. Fine-Tuning: Fine-tuning the baseline model on small emotional subsets of ESD.

All versions use the same text prompts to ensure content consistency. The models were trained and evaluated under low-resource settings to test emotional robustness.
